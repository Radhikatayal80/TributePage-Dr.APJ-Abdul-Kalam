Introduction

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, Indian scientist and honorable 11th president (2002-2007) of India, stepped into this world on October 15, 1931. He played a leading role in developing missile and nuclear weapon projects, which rewarded him with the nickname The Missile Man.

He was a very simple person who lived an unpretentious lifestyle. He had a keen interest in literature and wrote poems. He never married. He always faced the media himself for his failures while letting others address for the successes. He remained active till the very end, passing away while delivering a lecture at IIM.

Timeline

  • 1931 - Born to a Tamil Muslim family in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu.
  • 1954 - Graduated in Physics from Saint Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli.
  • 1955 - Studied aerospace engineering at Madras Institute of Technology.
  • 1960 - Joined the Aeronautical Development Establishment of DRDO as a scientist.
  • 1969 - Transferred to ISRO and became the project director of India's first SLV.
  • 1981 - Awarded Padma Bhushan.
  • 1990 - Awarded Padma Vibhushan.
  • 1997 - Awarded Bharat Ratna.
  • 2002 - Elected as the 11th President of India.
  • 2012 - Launched the What Can I Give Movement.
  • 2015 - Passed away while delivering a lecture at IIM Shillong.
